Tribute to APJ Abdul Kalam

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, known as the Missile Man of India, was an illustrious scientist and the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development.

He graduated in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology in 1960. After graduation, he joined the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) and later the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO).

Dr. Kalam was a key figure in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, which established India as a nuclear power. His dedication and vision made him a beloved figure across the nation, inspiring millions of young minds.

In addition to his scientific achievements, he authored several books including Wings of Fire, Ignited Minds, and India 2020, which continue to inspire people globally.

Dr. Kalam passed away on 27 July 2015, but his legacy lives on in the hearts of millions. He is remembered for his humility, inspirational speeches, and dedication to education and youth empowerment.
